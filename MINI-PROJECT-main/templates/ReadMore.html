What is a Malware? How to prevent it?

Malicious software, including viruses and Trojans, that infects devices to steal, damage, or disrupt data. Prevention: Install antivirus software, keep software updated, and avoid downloading from untrusted sources.

What are Ransomwares?

Attackers lock or encrypt your files, demanding payment for access to your data. Prevention: Regularly back up data, avoid clicking on untrusted email attachments, and keep systems updated with security patches..

What do you mean by DDoS?

(Distributed Denial of Service): Attackers flood a server with excessive traffic, causing service disruptions. Prevention: Use firewalls, load balancers, and DDoS protection services to mitigate large-scale traffic surges.

What is Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)?

Attackers inject malicious scripts into websites, affecting users who visit those sites. Prevention: Sanitize user inputs, use Content Security Policy (CSP), and encode output data to prevent script execution..

What is SQL Injection?

Attackers exploit insecure forms to manipulate databases and extract sensitive data. Prevention: Use parameterized queries, validate inputs, and implement strong access controls for database interaction..

What is Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks?

Attackers intercept communication between two parties to steal or alter information. Prevention: Use encryption protocols like SSL/TLS, avoid unsecured public Wi-Fi, and implement multi-factor authentication. .
